SOA Official Sample Questions

From the SOA Exam P Sample Questions document (680 questions, updated October 2025). Click an answer to reveal the solution.

1

General Probability — Set Theory

A survey of a group's viewing habits over the last year revealed: 28% watched gymnastics, 29% baseball, 19% soccer, 14% gymnastics and baseball, 12% baseball and soccer, 10% gymnastics and soccer, 8% all three. Calculate the percentage who watched none of the three sports.

Answer: (D) 52%
By inclusion-exclusion: $P(G\cup B\cup S) = 0.28+0.29+0.19-0.14-0.10-0.12+0.08 = 0.48$.
$P(\text{none}) = 1 - 0.48 = \mathbf{0.52 = 52\%}$.
2

General Probability — Inclusion-Exclusion

The probability a PCP visit results in neither lab work nor referral is 35%. Of PCP visits, 30% result in specialist referral and 40% require lab work. Calculate the probability a visit results in both lab work and a specialist referral.

Answer: (A) 0.05
Let $R$ = referral, $L$ = lab work. We know $P(R^c \cap L^c)=0.35$, so $P(R\cup L)=0.65$.
$P(R\cap L) = P(R)+P(L)-P(R\cup L) = 0.30+0.40-0.65 = \mathbf{0.05}$.
3

General Probability — Set Operations

You are given $P[A\cup B]=0.7$ and $P[A\cup B']=0.9$. Calculate $P[A]$.

Answer: (D) 0.6
Add the two equations: $P[A\cup B]+P[A\cup B'] = 0.7+0.9 = 1.6$.
Note $P[A\cup B]+P[A\cup B'] = 2P[A]+P[B]+P[B']-P[A\cap B]-P[A\cap B'] = 2P[A]+1-P[A] = P[A]+1$.
So $P[A]+1 = 1.6 \Rightarrow P[A] = \mathbf{0.6}$.
4

General Probability — Independence

Urn 1 has 4 red and 6 blue balls. Urn 2 has 16 red balls and an unknown number of blue balls. One ball is drawn from each urn. The probability both balls are the same color is 0.44. Calculate the number of blue balls in Urn 2.

Answer: (A) 4
Let $x$ = number of blue in Urn 2. Then:
$\frac{4}{10}\cdot\frac{16}{x+16}+\frac{6}{10}\cdot\frac{x}{x+16}=0.44$
$64+6x = 4.4(x+16) \Rightarrow 0.8x = 3.2 \Rightarrow x = \mathbf{4}$.
5

General Probability — Counting

An auto insurer has 10,000 policyholders. Of these: 3,000 young; 4,600 male; 7,000 married; 1,320 young male; 3,010 married male; 1,400 young married; 600 young married male. Calculate the number who are young, female, and single.

Answer: (D) 880
$N(\text{young female single}) = N(\text{young female}) - N(\text{young female married})$
$= [N(\text{young})-N(\text{young male})] - [N(\text{young married})-N(\text{young married male})]$
$= (3000-1320)-(1400-600) = 1680-800 = \mathbf{880}$.
6

General Probability — Conditional Probability

Medical records of 937 men: 210 died from heart disease; 312 had at least one parent with heart disease; of those 312, 102 died from heart disease. Calculate $P(\text{heart disease} \mid \text{no parental history})$.

Answer: (B) 0.173
Let $H$ = heart disease death, $F$ = parental history.
$P(H\cap F^c) = (210-102)/937 = 108/937$;  $P(F^c) = (937-312)/937 = 625/937$.
$P(H|F^c) = \dfrac{108/937}{625/937} = \dfrac{108}{625} = \mathbf{0.173}$.
7

General Probability — Total Probability

An insurer: 40% of auto-only holders renew, 60% of homeowners-only renew, 80% of combined holders renew at least one. Records: 65% auto policy, 50% homeowners, 15% both. Calculate the percentage of all policyholders that will renew at least one policy.

Answer: (D) 53%
$P(A\cap H^c)=0.65-0.15=0.50$;  $P(A^c\cap H)=0.50-0.15=0.35$;
Renewal = $0.4(0.50)+0.6(0.35)+0.8(0.15)=0.20+0.21+0.12=\mathbf{0.53}=53\%$.
8

General Probability — Inclusion-Exclusion

Among shoulder injury patients: 22% visit both a physical therapist and chiropractor; 12% visit neither. $P(\text{chiropractor})$ exceeds $P(\text{physical therapist})$ by 0.14. Calculate $P(\text{physical therapist})$.

Answer: (D) 0.48
$P(C)=P(T)+0.14$;  $P(C\cup T)=1-0.12=0.88$;
$P(C\cup T)=P(C)+P(T)-0.22=0.88 \Rightarrow P(C)+P(T)=1.10$;
Solving: $2P(T)+0.14=1.10 \Rightarrow P(T)=\mathbf{0.48}$.

SOA Official Sample Questions

From the SOA Exam FM Sample Questions document (462 questions, updated August 2024). Click an answer to reveal the solution.

1

Time Value of Money — Force of Interest

Bruce deposits 100 into a bank account at a nominal rate of 4% convertible semiannually. Peter deposits 100 at an annual force of interest $\delta$. After 7.25 years, both accounts have equal value. Calculate $\delta$.

Answer: (C) 0.0396
Equivalent rates: $e^\delta = \left(1+\frac{0.04}{2}\right)^2 = 1.02^2 = 1.0404$.
$\delta = \ln(1.0404) = \mathbf{0.0396}$.
2

Annuities — Accumulated Value

Kathryn deposits 100 at the beginning of each 4-year period for 40 years at annual effective rate $i$. The accumulated amount at the end of 40 years is $X$, which equals 5 times the accumulated amount at the end of 20 years. Calculate $X$.

Answer: (E) 6,195
Setting $x=(1+i)^4$, the ratio condition gives $x^{10}-5x^5+4=0$, factoring as $(x^5-1)(x^5-4)=0$.
Positive solution: $x^5=4 \Rightarrow x=1.31951$.
$X = 100\cdot\dfrac{1.31951-1.31951^{11}}{1-1.31951} = \mathbf{6195}$.
3

Time Value of Money — Nominal vs Simple Interest

Eric deposits 100 at nominal rate $i$ compounded semiannually. Mike deposits 200 at simple interest rate $i$. They earn the same interest during the last 6 months of year 8. Calculate $i$.

Answer: (C) 9.46%
Eric's interest (last 6 months of yr 8) = $100\left(1+\frac{i}{2}\right)^{15}\cdot\frac{i}{2}$.
Mike's interest (same period) = $200\cdot\frac{i}{2}$. Setting equal:
$\left(1+\frac{i}{2}\right)^{15}=2 \Rightarrow 1+\frac{i}{2}=2^{1/15}=1.04729 \Rightarrow i=\mathbf{9.46\%}$.
4

Annuities — Increasing Perpetuity

A perpetuity costs 77.1 with end-of-year payments: 1 at end of year 2, 2 at end of year 3, …, $n$ at end of year $(n+1)$; payments remain constant at $n$ thereafter. Annual effective rate is 10.5%. Calculate $n$.

Answer: (C) 19
$77.1 = v(I\ddot{a})_{\overline{n}|} = v\cdot\dfrac{\ddot{a}_{\overline{n}|}-nv^n}{i}$. At $i=0.105$, solving gives:
$1-v^n = 0.85003 \Rightarrow v^n=0.14997 \Rightarrow n = \dfrac{\ln(0.14997)}{\ln(1/1.105)} = \mathbf{19}$.
5

Cash Flows — Two Funds

1,000 is deposited into Fund X (6% annual effective). Each year-end: interest plus an additional 100 is withdrawn, depleting the fund after 10 years. All withdrawals are deposited into Fund Y (9% annual effective). Calculate Fund Y's accumulated value at end of year 10.

Answer: (C) 2,085
Interest withdrawals form a decreasing annuity (60, 54, …), plus 100 level withdrawals each year.
$AV_Y = 6(Ds)_{\overline{10}|0.09} + 100s_{\overline{10}|0.09} = 565.38 + 1519.29 = \mathbf{2084.67\approx 2085}$.
6

Loans — Amortization

A 20-year loan of 1,000 is repaid at 10% annual effective. The first 10 payments each equal 150% of interest due. Each of the last 10 payments is $X$. Calculate $X$.

Answer: (D) 97
Paying 150% of interest means 50% of interest reduces principal. After 10 years, balance = $1000(1-0.05)^{10} = 598.74$.
Level payment for last 10 years: $X = \dfrac{598.74}{a_{\overline{10}|10\%}} = \dfrac{598.74}{6.1446} = \mathbf{97.44\approx 97}$.
7

Bonds — Coupon Interest Portion

A 10,000 par value 10-year bond with 8% annual coupons is bought at a premium to yield 6% annual effective. Calculate the interest portion of the 7th coupon.

Answer: (B) 642
Book value at time 6: $BV_6 = 10000v^4 + 800a_{\overline{4}|6\%} = 7920.94 + 2772.08 = 10693$.
Interest portion of 7th coupon = $BV_6 \times i = 10693 \times 0.06 = \mathbf{641.58\approx 642}$.
8

Annuities — Geometrically Increasing

A perpetuity-immediate pays 100/year. After the 5th payment, it is exchanged for a 25-year annuity-immediate: first payment $X$, each subsequent payment 8% larger. Annual effective rate is 8%. Calculate $X$.

Answer: (A) 54
Perpetuity value after 5th payment = $100/0.08 = 1250$.
Since rate = growth rate = 8%, each $v\cdot(1.08)^{t-1}v^t = v$: annuity PV = $X\cdot 25/1.08$.
$1250 = 25X/1.08 \Rightarrow X = 1250\times 1.08/25 = \mathbf{54}$.
